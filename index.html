Research Interests

My goal is to build generative models of the visual world that are physically grounded, perceptually faithful, and useful to act on. I approach this from four angles that feed into one another:

  • Generative Video: physically-grounded, controllable video synthesis from a single image guided by simulation, so that generation obeys the dynamics of the real world such as heat, motion, and material behavior.
  • Perceptual Quality: subjective and objective quality assessment of rendered and generated content that turns what people actually perceive into a signal that systems can be measured against and optimize for.
  • Multimodal Perception: connecting vision with other modalities such as sound, touch, and language to model the physical world beyond what any single sense reveals.
  • Vision for Robotics: language and image-conditioned learning that turns perceptual understanding into action so that embodied agents can interpret scenes and act in real-world environments.
